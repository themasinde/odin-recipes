Braised Beef Recipe

What is Braised beef?

Braising is a cooking method that involves slowly cooking meat in a covered
pot with some sort of liquid. In the case of this recipe, chuck roast is braised
in a mixture of beef broth and red wine. Braising is similar to stewing, but it
requires less liquid.

Ingredients

  • 3 ½ pounds boneless beef chuck roast

  • 2 ½ teaspoons freshly ground black pepper, divided
  • 2 teaspoons garlic powder
  • 4 teaspoons olive oil

Steps

  1. Gather all ingredients and preheat the oven to 350 degrees F (175 degrees C).
  2. Season beef pieces evenly on all sides with 3 1/2 teaspoons salt, 2 teaspoons
    pepper, and garlic powder.
  3. Heat oil in a large skillet over high heat. Add beef, reduce heat to medium
    high and cook, undisturbed until it releases easily from the skillet and a
    golden brown crust has formed, about 4 minutes. Flip pieces and continue to
    cook until browned on all sides. Remove meat from the pan and set aside.
  4. Return skillet to medium-high heat and add 2 tablespoons butter to the
    drippings in the pan. When butter has melted add onion, mushrooms and carrots
    and cook 5 minutes, stirring often, scraping to release any browned bits from
    the bottom. Add garlic and cook for 1 minute.
  5. Stir in wine and scrape to release any more bits from the pan. Return the meat
    to the pan, along with any accumulated juices and add thyme, bay leaves and
    Worcestershire.
